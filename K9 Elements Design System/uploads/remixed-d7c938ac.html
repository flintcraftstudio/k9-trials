Build priorities for this flow
  • SportsEngine handles steps 4–7 natively — minimize custom forms here. The handoff back to K9E custom shell happens at step 8.
  • Step 10 ("First-time path") is the differentiator. Most sports drop newcomers into a generic dashboard; we route them based on their answers (do you have a dog? have you trained? do you compete elsewhere?).
  • Step 13 — the bridge from signup to actual training engagement — is where most platforms lose people. AI coach + live coaching booking + local club referral all need to be one click from the dashboard.

Build priorities for this flow
  • Steps 4–7 must execute fast and inline. If a member has a stale vaccination record, don't wait until checkout — flag it the moment they pick a dog.
  • Step 8b (missing data) is critical UX: never make the handler abandon their cart. Allow upload-and-proceed without leaving the page.
  • Step 13 — random running order at T-24h — is currently a paper-and-spreadsheet process at most clubs. Auto-draw with publish-to-portal eliminates a major host operations burden.

Build priorities for this flow
  • The 60-second budget is a hard design constraint. Every step beyond minimum sheds adoption.
  • Speech recognition can run on-device for privacy (Apple/Android native APIs); cloud APIs (Whisper) for higher accuracy. Suggest A/B testing both.
  • The "assigned roster" of step 3 needs explicit data modeling: club affiliation, seminar enrollment, paid coaching relationship — these all create a decoy↔handler edge that grants write access.
  • Audio file is preserved alongside transcript (members opt in). High-trust handlers may prefer to listen back to inflection rather than read.

Build priorities for this flow
  • The tablet UI must work fully offline. Spotty rural venues are common — sync when network returns.
  • Step 16 (live public results) is a recruiting tool. Spectators and family can follow runs in real time without reloading; live streaming integration ties video to scoresheets.
  • Title sheet generation (step 19) honors the stakeholder's "physical document for titles" requirement while everything around it is digital. Auto-generated PDF with judge signature, secretary signature line, dog photo, and trial details.
  • Auto-failure logic must be enforced at the moment a triggering event is logged (e.g. "dog left field") — not at submit. The judge gets a confirmation prompt: "This action triggers a non-qualifying score. Confirm?"

Build priorities for this flow
  • Every step has an SLA. Build automated reminders to reviewing directors at 50% and 80% of clock to prevent missed deadlines.
  • The grievance form should pull context automatically: trial, run number, judge, score. Reduces user friction and avoids ambiguity.
  • Audit trail is non-negotiable. Every state change logged with timestamp + actor for downstream legal review if needed.
  • Public-facing summary (step 18) only published when disciplinary action lands at moderate or above per rulebook. Privacy by default.

Build priorities for this flow
  • Variance tracking (step 13) is the killer feature — auto-compares apprentice scores vs mentor scores per criterion. Calibration becomes data-driven, not opinion-driven.
  • Step 21 — minimum participation — runs as a continuous background job. Officials get warnings at 75% of period if they're behind on requirements; auto-flags inactive at 100%.
  • Same flow shape applies to Trial Decoys and Development Decoys with different content gates. Build once, parameterize per role.
  • The platform stores video clips submitted for College review, indexed by reviewer + score variance. Over time this becomes the largest training dataset for AI scoring assistance.
