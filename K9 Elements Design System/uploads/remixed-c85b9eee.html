POST-SESSION NOTES, IN UNDER A MINUTE.

The biggest gap in working dog sport is institutional memory. Notes get scribbled on paper, lost, or never recorded at all. Handlers can't track patterns across decoys; decoys can't track patterns across handlers.

K9 Elements solves this with a mobile-first capture surface optimized for the post-session moment.

  • Voice-first. One-tap dictation. Transcription happens in seconds.
  • Auto-tagging. AI suggests tags based on transcript content; decoy confirms or edits.
  • Context-aware. Decoy selects handler from their assigned roster; pulls last session for reference.
  • Permission-aware. Notes flow to the handler's development log immediately, scoped to the dog and session — no leak between handlers.
  • Search-aware. Tags become filters on the handler's profile (e.g. "show me everything about out commands").
Why this matters The development log becomes a living, searchable record that follows the handler-dog team across years and across multiple decoys. A new decoy can read 18 months of context in 5 minutes.
Designer notes
  • This screen needs a true mobile build — likely a PWA or native shell — not a responsive crunch of the desktop site. The post-session moment is field-time, often outdoors, often with one hand. Voice-first is non-negotiable.
  • Transcription provider (e.g. Whisper API or Deepgram) needs evaluation; tag-suggestion can be a lighter-weight call to the AI coach model with a tag taxonomy in the prompt.
  • Privacy: a development decoy can only see/write notes for handlers they're assigned to (via the club affiliation or seminar registration). This is an access-tier rule, not a UI rule.

Disciplines · Obedience · Level 1

§7.2 LEVEL 1 — FOUNDATION OBEDIENCE

What you'll need to know Level 1 tests four phases — Muzzle & Stability, Area Search, Obstacles, and Retrieve — for a total of 100 points. Pass threshold is 70. This is the foundation level: it's about communication, control, and the dog's neutrality.

Pass Criteria

  • 70+ points out of 100
  • One qualifying score earns the K9E1-OB title
  • No automatic failure triggers (see §4.5)

Phase 1 — Muzzle & Stability (25 pts)

Equipment: 3–4' leash, flat or fur saver collar.

Purpose: Demonstrate handler control, dog stability, and neutrality in a muzzled state. This phase tests safety, culture, and the dog's ability to remain calm under mild social pressure.

  • Heeling with Muzzle — 2 turns + 1 halt. Position, engagement, neutrality despite muzzle.
  • Muzzle Stability — Dog remains calm while steward approaches handler with questions.
  • Long Stay — 30-second sit or down stay, handler 10 m away.
  • Food Refusal — Post-muzzle. Impulse control to passive food presentation.

Designer notes
  • Rulebook content lives as structured data (not a PDF). Every section, sub-section, scoresheet criterion, and deduction rule has its own ID. This is what powers the scoring tablet and the AI coach.
  • Cross-references render as live chips — tapping "§4.5 Automatic Failures" navigates contextually without losing place. A "Related" surface at the bottom of every page connects rules to handbooks, videos, and example scoresheets.
  • The AI coach FAB bottom-right is contextual: it knows which section the user is reading and answers questions scoped to that rule. ("If my dog breaks the long stay, do I lose all 25 points or just the long stay points?")
  • Version control is essential — when the rulebook updates, members need diff views and changelog entries. The "UPDATED" badge in the TOC flags recently changed sections.
